Research

Thin Film Mesoscopic Solar Cells

Our group combines fundamental and applied research to optimize each component of this mesoscopic solar energy conversion architecture, i.e. liquid, quasi-solid and solid state DSSC, quantum dots solar cell and perovskite solar cell, to improve the systemâ€™s performance.

Nano Antimicrobial Materials

We developed nanomaterials with superior bactericidal effect toward wide range of bacterial strains by exploiting plasmonic nanoparticle, metal oxide nanoparticle, and various carbon allotrope. Our group is also interested in the understanding of antibacterial mechanism and the corresponding kinetics.

Paper-Based Microfluidic Biosensor

Our group focuses on the geometrical optimization of paper-based multichannel separation system, the screening of various techniques (spectroscopy, electrochemistry, colorimetry) to detect targeted bio-substance, and the development of portable detection system using paper-based microfluidic biosensor.

Catalyst and Photocatalysis

Our group develop a wide range of wet chemical synthetic route to prepare photocatalyst based on metal oxide semiconductor (ZnO, TiO2, WO3, NiO, CuO), quantum dots, nanocarbonaceous materials, WS2, CdSe, etc. The group devotes our focus to design and develop photocatalytic reactors.

Renewable Energy & Sustainable Environment

As part of interdisciplinary study dedicated to renewable energy and sustainable environment, our group in collaboration with our partner at ITB Bandung develops renewable energy potential map (Solar, Hidro, Wind, Biomass, Ocean) using GIS, climate data and hydrological model.

Resources & Services

UV/visible absorption and emission spectrometer

UV/visible absorption and emission spectrometer (Ocean Optics and Vernier) to determine extinction coefficient of molecules, estimate Eg of semiconductor, emission quantum yield.

Potentiostat (VersaSTAT)

Potentiostat (VersaSTAT) for electrochemical and spectroelectrochemical method to probe corrosion behaviour and charge transfer process at the photoelectrode interface, respectively.

Raman spectrometer

Raman spectrometer (excitation wavelength 785 nm, StellarNet) and Raman spectrometer (excitation wavelength 532 nm, ThunderOptics) to probe properties of materials, e.g. chemical structure and identity, phase and polymorphism, Intrinsic stress/strain, and contamination and impurity.

Solar simulator

Solar simulator (Class AAA equipped with AM1.5 Filter) with source meter to characterize current-voltage (I-V) response of solar cell.

Controllable spin coater

Controllable spin coater for thin film deposition
